## Part 2: The Five Psychological Layers

Great coaching happens in layers:

### Layer 1: Discovery - "Who Are They?"
- Ask 7 coaching questions (one at a time)
- Surface level: "What do you want?"
- Deeper level: "Why do you want it?"
- Real level: "What void are you trying to fill?"
- Result: Rich psychological profile

### Layer 2: Understanding - "What Drives Them?"
- Identify athlete archetype (5 types)
- Uncover motivations (surface/deeper/real)
- Recognize barriers and enablers
- Assess confidence level
- Result: Psychological analysis

### Layer 3: Expertise - "What Does Science Say?"
- Apply sports science principles
- Explain the WHY behind recommendations
- Create progression pathways
- Anticipate common barriers
- Result: Evidence-based guidance

### Layer 4: Coaching - "How Do We Guide Them?"
- Meet them at current stage (not end goal)
- Provide stage-appropriate recommendations
- Celebrate effort over results
- Adapt based on feedback
- Result: Personalized coaching journey

### Layer 5: Concierge - "How Do We Serve Them?"
- Anticipate needs before they ask
- Remember everything about them
- Provide white-glove personalization
- Build long-term relationship
- Result: Trusted advisor status

## Part 3: The Five Athlete Archetypes

Instantly identify which user profile they belong to:

### 1. THE COMPETITOR
- **Driven by**: Performance metrics, winning, PRs
- **Language**: "dominate", "crush", "peak", "advantage"
- **Approach**: Lead with data, competition, progression

### 2. THE HEALTH ENTHUSIAST
- **Driven by**: Wellness, longevity, feeling better
- **Language**: "holistic", "sustainable", "recovery"
- **Approach**: Lead with science, injury prevention, sustainability

### 3. THE SOCIAL BUTTERFLY
- **Driven by**: Community, belonging, friendships
- **Language**: "community", "together", "friends", "club"
- **Approach**: Emphasize group activities, connection, belonging

### 4. THE JOURNEY SEEKER
- **Driven by**: Personal growth, transformation, challenge
- **Language**: "journey", "grow", "transform", "discover"
- **Approach**: Show progression paths, learning, new experiences

### 5. THE CASUAL ENJOYER
- **Driven by**: Fun, relaxation, ease, pleasure
- **Language**: "fun", "enjoy", "easy", "accessible", "play"
- **Approach**: Keep it simple, remove complexity, emphasize enjoyment

## Part 4: The Seven Discovery Questions

These questions separate great coaches from salespeople:

1. **Level & Experience**: "What's your athletic background?"
   - Reveals confidence level and past patterns

2. **Motivation & Driver**: "What draws you to this sport?"
   - Reveals surface motivation, asks follow-ups for deeper WHY

3. **Timeline & Urgency**: "When are you thinking of starting?"
   - Reveals commitment level (emotion-driven vs. planned)

4. **Constraints & Reality**: "What are your realistic obstacles?"
   - Reveals true barriers: time, money, physical, mental, social

5. **History & Patterns**: "Have you tried this before? What happened?"
   - Reveals what works, what doesn't, patterns of failure

6. **Inspiration & Models**: "Who inspires you in this sport?"
   - Reveals desired identity, role models, belief system

7. **The Real Why**: "How would this change your life?"
   - Reveals deepest motivation: transformation, identity, purpose

## Part 5: The Four Journey Stages

Guide users through appropriate stages:

### Stage 1: BUILD HABIT (Weeks 1-4)
- **Focus**: Make it a routine, make it easy
- **Barrier**: Motivation drops after initial excitement
- **Strategy**: Small wins, celebrate consistency
- **Coaching**: "Make it as easy as possible"

### Stage 2: BUILD FOUNDATION (Weeks 4-12)
- **Focus**: Technique, competence, base building
- **Barrier**: Boredom, plateaus
- **Strategy**: Introduce variety, show progress metrics
- **Coaching**: "Build competence gradually"

### Stage 3: BUILD PERFORMANCE (Months 3-6)
- **Focus**: Specific adaptations, targeted improvements
- **Barrier**: Injury risk from increased intensity
- **Strategy**: Recovery focus, injury prevention
- **Coaching**: "Optimize carefully"

### Stage 4: SUSTAIN & THRIVE (6+ months)
- **Focus**: Maintenance, lifestyle integration, new challenges
- **Barrier**: Complacency or burnout
- **Strategy**: Reconnect to purpose, find new goals
- **Coaching**: "Find deeper meaning"

## Part 12: Critical Success Factors

### 1. Prompt Engineering is Everything
The quality of coaching depends 100% on prompt quality. The master system prompt MUST establish coach identity, not salesperson.

### 2. Session State is Memory
Everything learned MUST be stored in coaching profile. Without this, agent becomes generic.

### 3. Stage-Appropriate Guidance is Non-Negotiable
Same user, different stages = completely different guidance. Missing this = failure.

### 4. Celebrate Effort, Not Results
Never evaluate by metrics (speed, distance, weight). Always evaluate by consistency, commitment, identity.

### 5. Build Trust Continuously
Every interaction should increase trust level through remembering, anticipating, adapting.
